Top 10 Movies with Lesbian Representation

  • 1. But I'm a Cheerleader (1999)
    Megan (Natasha Lyonne) considers herself a typical American girl. She excels in school and cheerleading, and she has a handsome football-playing boyfriend, even though she isn't that crazy about him. So she's stunned when her parents decide she's gay and send her to True Directions, a boot camp meant to alter her sexual orientation. While there, Megan meets a rebellious and unashamed teen lesbian, Graham (Clea DuVall). Though Megan still feels confused, she starts to have feelings for Graham.
  • 2. Carol (2015)
    Carol, directed by Todd Haynes, is a romantic drama set in 1950s New York. It follows Therese (Rooney Mara), an aspiring photographer, and Carol (Cate Blanchett), a glamorous older woman trapped in a loveless marriage. As the two women form a deep connection, their love faces societal pressures and personal obstacles. Carol's custody battle for her daughter complicates their relationship, forcing her to make difficult choices. The film explores themes of love, desire, and identity against the backdrop of a repressive era.
  • 3. The Handmaiden (2016)
    The Handmaiden is based on the lesbian historical novel Fingersmith by Sarah Waters—but set in 1930s Korea instead of Victorian Britain—about a young con artist, Sook-Hee (Kim Tae-ri), who conspires to help a man rob and institutionalize a rich heiress, Lady Hideko (Kim Min-Hee). Instead, she stumbles upon an abusive secret and ends up falling in love with her planned victim. The film explores themes of love, betrayal, and power, with a series of shocking twists.
  • 4. Pariah (2011)
    Pariah follows 17-year-old Alike (Adepero Oduye), who has been going to queer clubs with her openly lesbian friend Laura (Pernell Walker). Alike, however, is not entirely sure where she belongs. When she finally comes out to her parents, she must grapple with their different reactions to her identity, including violence. It's a real coming-of-age story that roots for Alike the whole way. The film explores themes of identity, self-discovery, and acceptance in a world filled with familial and societal pressures.
  • 5. The Miseducation of Cameron Post (2018)
    The Miseducation of Cameron Post, directed by Desiree Akhavan, follows Cameron (Chloe Grace Moretz), a teenager sent to a gay conversion therapy camp after being caught in a same-sex relationship. At the camp, she meets other LGBTQ+ teens and forms bonds with those who, like her, are pressured to change their identities. As Cameron navigates the camp's oppressive environment, she questions the validity of the therapy and fights for her sense of self. The film explores themes of identity, conformity, and resilience.
  • 6. Saving Face (2004)
    Saving Face, directed by Alice Wu, is a romantic comedy-drama about Wilhelmina (Michelee Krusiec), a Chinese-American surgeon navigating her closeted lesbian identity and complex family dynamics. When her unwed, pregnant mother moves in, Wil must balance caring for her while keeping her relationship with Vivian (Lynn Chen), a dancer, a secret from their traditional community. As both women confront societal and cultural expectations, they struggle to embrace love and authenticity. The film explores themes of cultural identity, family, and acceptance.
  • 7. Pride (2014)
    Pride, directed by Matthew Warchus, is a British historical comedy-drama based on true events. Set in 1984, it follows a group of LGBTQ+ activists who form "Lesbians and Gays Support the Miners" to raise money for striking Welsh miners during the UK miners' strike. Despite initial resistance, the two communities unite in solidarity, overcoming prejudice and forging unlikely friendships. The film celebrates the power of allyship and collective action, highlighting themes of equality, activism, and the fight for human rights.
  • 8. The Half of It (2020)
    This Netflix movie, directed by Alice Wu, follows a high school student named Ellie Chu (Leah Lewis), who earns money ghostwriting essays for her classmates. One day, popular boy Paul Munsky (Daniel Diemer) asks her to help him write a love letter to Aster Flores (Alexxis Lemire). He and Ellie actually have something in common: they both have a crush on Aster. Despite her feelings, Ellie agrees, and soon the three of them are embroiled in a love triangle that won't be easily settled.
  • 9. Portrait of a Lady on Fire (2019)
    Portrait of a Lady on Fire, directed by Céline Sciamma, is a French period romance set in the late 18th century. It follows Marianne (Noemie Merlant), a painter hired to secretly create a portrait of Héloïse (Adele Haenel), a young woman promised in marriage. As Marianne observes Héloïse, the two women develop a deep emotional and romantic bond. Their forbidden love flourishes within the confines of isolation, exploring themes of art, memory, and the female gaze. The film is a haunting meditation on love and loss.
  • 10. Disobedience (2017)
    Disobedience chronicles a story of forbidden love between two women from a conservative religious community. When Ronit’s (Rachel Weisz) father dies, she travels to London and returns to the Orthodox Jewish community of her upbringing—the community that ostracized her years ago after catching her with another woman. Once there, she’s startled to discover that the woman she was caught with, Esti (Rachel McAdams), is now married to their childhood friend Dovid (Alessandro Nivola).
